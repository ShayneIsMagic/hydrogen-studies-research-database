Hydrogen attenuates endothelial glycocalyx damage associated with partial cardiopulmonary bypass in rats

Published: March 15, 2020 Journal: PLoS One Authors: Zhang Y, Liu B, Chen X, Liu H DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0231928

Abstract

Background: Cardiopulmonary bypass (CPB) is associated with endothelial glycocalyx damage. This study investigated whether hydrogen could protect the endothelial glycocalyx during partial CPB in rats.

Methods: Rats were randomly assigned to control, CPB, or CPB+H2 groups. In the CPB and CPB+H2 groups, partial CPB was established. In the CPB+H2 group, hydrogen-rich saline was administered. Endothelial glycocalyx markers, inflammatory factors, and oxidative stress indicators were measured.

Results: CPB significantly increased plasma levels of glycocalyx degradation products and inflammatory markers. Hydrogen treatment significantly attenuated these changes and preserved glycocalyx integrity.

Conclusions: Hydrogen protects endothelial glycocalyx during partial CPB, potentially through its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
